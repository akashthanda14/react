# What Executives & VCs Actually Want to Know

Great question, Akashdeep — and this is a lens shift most engineers don't think about. Here's how decision-makers actually evaluate a startup's product/tech:

---

## 1. Problem-Solution Fit
Does this solve a real, painful problem? They want to see the "before vs after" — not your architecture diagram. They care about *what changes* for the end user or business.

---

## 2. Core Product Demo (Not a Feature Tour)
They want to see the **critical user journey** — the one flow that delivers the core value. Not 47 features. Just: "Here's the problem → here's how we solve it → here's the result." Under 5 minutes.

---

## 3. Traction & Usage Metrics
- DAU/MAU, retention curves, churn rate
- Time-to-value (how fast does a new user get the "aha" moment?)
- Revenue or pipeline if applicable
- NPS or qualitative user feedback

---

## 4. Technical Differentiation (High Level)
They don't want to hear "we use Kubernetes." They want to know:
- What's your **moat**? Proprietary data, algorithm, network effect?
- How hard is this to replicate?
- What's the **10x advantage** over existing solutions?

---

## 5. Scalability & Reliability Signals
- Can it handle 10x current load?
- What's the uptime track record?
- Any enterprise-grade compliance (SOC2, GDPR)?

---

## 6. Integration & Ecosystem Fit
- Does it plug into tools they already use? (Slack, Salesforce, AWS, etc.)
- API availability, SSO support
- How painful is onboarding?

---

## 7. Team & Execution Capability
- Who built this? Domain expertise matters.
- Shipping velocity — how fast do they iterate?
- Technical debt vs. speed tradeoff awareness

---

## 8. Unit Economics & GTM
- CAC vs LTV
- Pricing model clarity
- Who's the buyer vs. the user?

---

## 9. Security & Risk
- Where does data live? Who has access?
- Vendor lock-in risk
- What happens if the startup dies? (Data portability)

---

## The Mental Model

> Executives evaluate tech through the lens of **risk, ROI, and speed to value** — not through architectural elegance.
> VCs add **market size and defensibility** on top of that.
